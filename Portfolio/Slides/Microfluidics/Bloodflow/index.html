The Blood

An artificial substitute for human blood has not been found

The Red Blood Cells(RBC)

  • 45% of blood volume
  • Biconcave shape
  • $8\mu m$ diameter
  • Highly deformable
  • Transport $O_2$ and $CO_2$
  • Lifetime $\tau = 120$ days

Why Study Blood Flow?

  • Transport function: Blood is 4.5 - 5.5 times more viscous than water.
  • Flow through narrow capillaries, with diameter several times smaller than RBC size.
  • Understand biophysical behavior of blood flow in vessels.
  • Design microfluidic devices for decease diagnosis.
Hemodynamics: blood motion and equilibrium under the action of external forces
Is the study of blood flow or the circulation. It explains the physical laws that govern the flow of blood in the blood vessels.

Microfluidic Modeling of Cell-Cell Interactions in Malaria Pathogenesis

    Investigation of:
  • human infections by Plasmodium falciparum – the pathogen responsible only for 15% of deceases but for 90% of fatal cases.
  • studying the in vitro model for malaria pathogenesis, employing microfluidics which allows for flow experiments in channels that have the same dimensions as capillaries in the microvasculature, using very small sample volumes,
  • how to link the adhesive interactions between host ligands (ICAM-1) and parasitized erythrocytes with the unique effects of blood flow in capillary-like environments.

ICAM-1 may be particularly important for mediating cytoadhesion in the brain.
Without ICAM-1, binding to the endothelium under flow conditions is impaired.

Conclusions

  1. Microfluidic devices can be fabricated to identify and possibly select cell types that will most likely stabilize rolling.
  2. Taking advantage of microfluidic devices that are portable and require microliter volumes of samples, future applications should be possible at field sites, using patient samples.
  3. The microfluidic devices can be mounted on a microscope, and data on single cells can be collected as still photos or as movies on a personal computer for further detailed analysis.
  4. The ability of microfluidic devices to support both pure ligands and ligand-expressing human cells in culture, under high shear stress, should facilitate a detailed understanding of the role of the ligand environment in precipitation of capillary blockage in the microvasculature.
M. Antia, T. Herricks, P.K. Pradipsinh, PLOS Pathogens, 3(7), e99,(2007)
